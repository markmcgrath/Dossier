# Scoring Calibration Guide

Use this as a reference when scoring the 10 evaluation dimensions. The goal is consistency — a "3" should mean the same thing across evaluations.

---

## Score Definitions

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | Excellent | Near-perfect fit or clear strength — little to no gap |
| 4 | Good | Solid fit with minor gaps or uncertainties |
| 3 | Moderate | Partial fit — some alignment, some meaningful gaps |
| 2 | Weak | Significant mismatch or gaps that would be hard to overcome |
| 1 | Poor | Fundamental mismatch — almost no alignment |

---

## Dimension-by-Dimension Calibration

### 1. Role & Responsibility Match (20%, gate-pass)
Does the day-to-day work described in the JD match what the user has been doing and wants to do?

- **5:** Core responsibilities are nearly identical to the user's recent experience
- **4:** 70–80% overlap; a few new areas but nothing unreasonable
- **3:** ~50% overlap; some responsibilities are new or different enough to require a learning curve
- **2:** Mostly new territory — the user would be doing things they've rarely or never done
- **1:** Fundamentally different function or domain

### 2. Skills & Experience Alignment (20%, gate-pass)
Do the required skills and experience level match the user's background?

- **5:** Required skills are the user's core stack; years of experience match
- **4:** Most required skills present; 1–2 minor gaps that are learnable
- **3:** Core skills present but 2–3 notable gaps, or experience level is off by ~1 year
- **2:** Multiple required skills missing; or significantly under/over-experienced
- **1:** Mismatched domain (e.g., backend engineer applying for data science role without signals)

### 3. Seniority & Level Fit (10%)
Is the role pitched at the right career level for the user?

- **5:** Exact level match — the role is squarely where the user operates today
- **4:** Slight stretch (one level up) or slight step back (one level down) with clear rationale
- **3:** Meaningful stretch or step back — manageable but notable
- **2:** Two levels off in either direction
- **1:** Extreme mismatch (e.g., IC role for someone with 15 years of leadership, or VP role for someone 2 years in)

### 4. Compensation vs. Market (10%)
Is the compensation fair and competitive for the role/location/level?

- **5:** Clearly above market or top-of-range for the user's experience
- **4:** Market rate — fair and expected
- **3:** Slightly below market but within acceptable range; or compensation not disclosed
- **2:** Meaningfully below market, or vague signals suggesting low offers
- **1:** Clearly underpaying for the level and market

### 5. Growth & Advancement Potential (10%)
Does the role offer meaningful opportunities to grow in skills, scope, or seniority?

- **5:** Clear path forward — explicit growth track, expanding team, high-leverage work
- **4:** Solid growth signals — good scope, likely advancement, interesting problems
- **3:** Growth possible but not obvious — depends on performance and timing
- **2:** Limited growth signals — narrow scope, mature/shrinking org, or dead-end signals
- **1:** Role likely leads nowhere — maintenance work, declining function, or shrinking company

### 6. Company Strength & Stability (10%)
Is the company healthy and positioned to be a good employer?

- **5:** Strong brand, healthy financials or funding, growing, stable leadership
- **4:** Good company with minor uncertainties (e.g., mid-stage startup, competitive market)
- **3:** Mixed signals — some positive, some concerning (recent layoffs, funding uncertainty)
- **2:** Concerning signals — financial instability, major leadership churn, shrinking headcount
- **1:** Red flags across the board — likely distressed company

### 7. Culture & Values Signals (8%)
Do the company's culture signals match what the user likely values?

- **5:** Strong alignment on working style, values, and culture based on available signals
- **4:** Good fit — minor unknowns but nothing concerning
- **3:** Some signals, but culture is hard to read or contains mild yellow flags
- **2:** Culture signals don't match the user's style, or Glassdoor / news signals are negative
- **1:** Clear culture mismatch or widespread negative signals

### 8. Remote / Location & Flexibility Fit (5%)
Does the work location, remote policy, AND work-style flexibility match the user's situation?

- **5:** Perfect match — fully remote when user is remote, plus strong flexibility signals (async culture, flexible hours, generous PTO)
- **4:** Mostly compatible — hybrid that works, or good location fit with moderate flexibility
- **3:** Requires some compromise (e.g., more in-office than ideal, or rigid schedule expectations)
- **2:** Significant friction — requires relocation or substantially changes working arrangement; or role demands on-site presence that conflicts with user preferences
- **1:** Non-starter — requires relocating to an unwanted location or fully in-office when remote is needed

**Flexibility signals to assess:** schedule autonomy, async vs. sync culture, PTO/leave policy, timezone requirements, travel expectations. These are distinct from geographic location and should be weighted in the score even when the location itself is a match.

### 9. Hiring Signal Quality (4%)
Does the job posting itself suggest a real, well-run hiring process?

- **5:** Clear, well-written JD; realistic requirements; specific responsibilities; named team
- **4:** Good JD with minor vagueness
- **3:** Generic JD — could be for anyone; or requirements list is suspiciously long
- **2:** Laundry-list requirements; vague role; reposted multiple times; or "ghost job" indicators
- **1:** Clear ghost job signals: extreme requirement mismatch, months reposted, template-only content

### 10. Strategic Career Value (3%)
Does taking this role move the user's career forward in a meaningful way?

- **5:** Clear strategic win — opens doors, builds rare skills, or puts user in a stronger position
- **4:** Solid move — reasonable step forward even if not transformational
- **3:** Neutral — maintains trajectory but doesn't accelerate it
- **2:** Slight step back — might close more doors than it opens
- **1:** Career-damaging move — would set the user back significantly

## Grade Conversion

| Weighted Score | Grade | Meaning |
|---------------|-------|---------|
| 4.50 – 5.00 | A | Strong yes |
| 4.00 – 4.49 | B+ | Very good fit |
| 3.50 – 3.99 | B | Worth pursuing |
| 3.00 – 3.49 | C | Apply selectively |
| 2.00 – 2.99 | D | Proceed with caution |
| < 2.00 | F | Not recommended |

**Gate-pass rule:** If Dimension 1 or Dimension 2 scores 2 or below, cap the final grade at D — even if other dimensions score high. The role or skills mismatch is too fundamental to overcome.
