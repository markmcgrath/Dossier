## Audit Summary

The quality audit scored the vault at **82/100** overall. The scoring framework, legitimacy detection, and grading integrity are strong. The gaps cluster into four themes:

1. **Schema & consistency** — frontmatter drift between eval vintages, missing fields, grade/heading mismatch
2. **Missing analytical dimensions** — no source/channel tracking, no referral contacts, no conversion funnel, no compensation trend
3. **Process gaps** — no follow-up cadence, no bias checks, no burnout monitoring, thin templates when data is sparse
4. **Template parity** — real evals lack the 10-dimension table and bias caveat that the example template includes

This plan is organized into four streams (A–D), ordered by impact on decision quality. Each stream can be executed independently.

### A.2 — Add Bias Caveat Section to Eval Output Template

**What:** Add a "Bias Caveat" section after the 10-dimension table, before the AI disclaimer, in the Mode 1 output template.

**Why this matters:** The example eval includes this section; real evals omit it. NIH, Decision Lab, and UNC Executive Development research identifies confirmation bias, anchoring, and the halo effect as the most dangerous biases in job search. An explicit bias caveat forces the evaluator (the model) to acknowledge what it cannot assess and prompts the user to check their own assumptions.

**Steps:**
1. Add the following to the output template in `references/mode1-offer-evaluator.md`, after the scoring table:

```markdown
## Bias Caveat

This evaluation does not account for:
- Actual manager/team quality (unknown without insider information)
- Internal org dynamics, recent reorgs, or undisclosed headcount changes
- Personal energy or enthusiasm for this role vs. others in the pipeline
- Anchoring effects from previously evaluated roles

If this role is from a prestigious company, check: are dimensions 6–7 inflated by brand recognition?
If this role scores lower than a previous favorite, check: is the comparison anchored on that earlier eval?
```

**Acceptance:**
- The template includes the bias caveat
- New evals include the section with role-specific bias checks where applicable

### D.3 — Consider Splitting Flexibility from Location in Scoring

**What:** Evaluate whether Dimension 8 (Remote / Location Fit, 5%) should be split into two dimensions: (a) geographic location match and (b) work-style flexibility (schedule autonomy, async culture, PTO policy).

**Why this matters:** A 2025 SAGE Journals study found flexibility is now a top-2 priority for job seekers, overtaking career growth. The current system folds flexibility into location at only 5% weight.

**Steps:**
1. This is a design decision, not a mechanical fix. Evaluate the trade-off:
   - **Pro:** Flexibility deserves more weight per research. Splitting creates a dedicated signal.
   - **Con:** Adding an 11th dimension increases complexity. The 10-dimension system is already well-calibrated.
   - **Alternative:** Keep 10 dimensions but increase Dimension 8 weight to 8% (taking 3% from Dimension 10, Strategic Career Value) and rename it "Remote, Location & Flexibility Fit." Update the scoring guide to explicitly include schedule/async/PTO signals in the calibration.

2. Whichever approach is chosen, update `references/scoring-guide.md` to include flexibility-specific calibration examples.

**Acceptance:**
- A decision is documented (split, reweight, or status quo)
- `scoring-guide.md` includes flexibility signals in Dimension 8 calibration regardless of the decision

### D.4 — Add Repost Fingerprinting to Legitimacy (Deferred)

**What:** Add JD text fingerprinting to detect when the same listing is reposted periodically with a new date. This requires comparing current JD text against previously evaluated JDs.

**Why this matters:** Periodic reposting of identical text is a strong ghost job indicator (Interview Guys checklist signal #6). The current dedup check catches same-company re-evals but does not compare JD text across time.

**Steps:**
- This is deferred until the vault has 60+ evals (sufficient corpus for meaningful fingerprinting). When implemented:
  1. On each eval, compute a normalized text fingerprint of the JD (lowercase, strip whitespace, hash first 500 characters)
  2. Compare against fingerprints of previous evals from any company
  3. If a match is found with a different company name or date >30 days apart, flag as a legitimacy warning

**Acceptance (deferred):**
- Fingerprinting is implemented and tested against the `tests/fixtures/` ghost job samples
- False positive rate is <5%

**Why this matters:** Ontario's Working for Workers Act (effective January 2026) and New York's proposed legislation (effective February 2026) create new transparency requirements. Mentioning these in legitimacy warnings for roles in those jurisdictions adds useful context.

**Steps:**
- Deferred until geographic scope of the job search includes these jurisdictions. When implemented:
  1. In the legitimacy assessment, check the role's location
  2. If Ontario or New York, add: *"Note: [Ontario/New York] ghost job legislation requires employers to disclose whether this position is being actively filled. If the posting lacks this disclosure, it may be non-compliant."*

**Acceptance (deferred):**
